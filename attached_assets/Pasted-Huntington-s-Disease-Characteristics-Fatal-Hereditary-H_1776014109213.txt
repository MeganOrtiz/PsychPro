Huntington's Disease 
 
-Characteristics: 
Fatal 
Hereditary  
Hyperkinetic  
-Onset  
30-50 
Can range from 2-85 
Men and women effected equally 
-Pathophysiology  
Single autosomal dominate mutation in gene  
HTT gene on chromosome 4 
CAG repeats 
Healthy- 10-27 
Intermediate- 27-35 
Severe- 36-50 or more  
Abnormally long glutamine stretch 
Polyglutamine (PolyQ) 
-Neuropathophysiology 
Neuronal death  
Cellular toxicity 
Excitotoxicity 
Excessive glutamate 
Excessive influx of calcium 
Mitochondrial dysfunction  
Protein aggregation and misfolding 
-Neuroanatomy 
Basal ganglia implicated  
Degeneration of the caudate 
Box-car ventricles  
As the caudate degenerates, the ventricles fill the space  
Striatum (caudate and putamen) 
Motor functioning 
Regulation of voluntary movements  
Cortex 
Frontal 
EF deficits  
Attention and concentration 
Organizing information  
No semantic clustering bc organization issues  
More recency and primary strategies  
Memory 
Encoding and retrieval deficits 
Source  
Parietal  
Visual-spatial deficits  
Before motor  
Hippocampus 
Can have consolidation deficits later  
-Symptoms 
Motor 
Chorea 
Dystonia 
Dysphasia/dysarthria 
Motor impersistence  
Cognitive  
Memory loss 
Difficulty making decisions 
Loss of judgement  
ADLs 
Dementia in advanced stages  
-Psychiatric Presentation 
Often precede motor issues  
Depression (most common) 
Anxiety 
OCD symptoms  
Apathy and irritability 
Psychosis  
-Behavioral Presentation 
Impulsivity and risk-taking 
Aggression 
Social withdrawal 
-Neuroimaging  
PET 
Shows changes in glucose metabolism  
-Neuropsych findings

Motor  
-Stages 
5 
Early to advanced 
-Outcomes

-Pediatric Huntington's
