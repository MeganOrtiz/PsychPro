Cranial Nerves 
I. Olfactory (Sensory) 
Smell 
Injury: anosmia 
Exit: cribriform plate 
II. Optic (Sensory) 
Vision and visual acuity 
Exit: optic canal 
III. Oculomotor (Motor) 
Eye movement and pupil constriction and dilation 
Exit: superior orbital fissure 
IV. Trochlear (Motor) 
Vertical eye movement 
Exit: superior orbital fissure 
V. Trigeminal (Motor) 
Face and mouth movement 
Nerve that's numbed when at the dentist 
Exit: superior orbital fissure/foramen rotundum/foramen ovale 
VI. Abducens (Sensory/Motor) 
Lateral eye movements 
Exit: superior orbital fissure 
VII. Facial (Motor) 
Facial movement and lacrimal/salivary function 
Exit: auditory canal 
VIII. Vestibulocochlear (Sensory) 
Balance and homeostatic function 
Exit: auditory canal 
IX. Glossopharyngeal (Sensory/Motor) 
Pharynx and swallowing function 
Exit: jugular foramen 
X. Vagus (Sensory/Motor) 
Visceral organs and glands; heart rate; uvula 
Exit: jugular foramen 
XI. Accessory (Motor) 
Rotation of head from side to side, also neck movement; allows you to do shrugs at the gym (traps) 
Exit: jugular foramen 
Hypoglossal (Motor) 
Movement of your tongue rather than the taste 
Exit: hypoglossal foramen
