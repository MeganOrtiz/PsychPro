B-2. FREE-TIER GATING (server-side enforced).

(a) FLASHCARDS — cap at 10 for free users.
Backend flashcards-by-topic endpoint: if caller's tier is free,
return only the first 10 cards (by cardOrder) PLUS a field like
{ totalCount: N, lockedCount: N-10, isCapped: true }. Master/
Scholar get all cards, isCapped:false. The remaining cards must
NOT be in the free response payload (server-side protection).
Frontend flashcards.tsx: after the 10th card, show an upgrade
card ("You've previewed 10 of N cards. Unlock the full deck with
Master.") instead of more cards.

(b) QUIZZES — 1 completed quiz total for free users.
Track completed quizzes server-side. Add a count/flag (e.g.
users.freeQuizzesCompleted or a small free_quiz_completions
record). On quiz COMPLETION (the existing recordAttempt call),
if free tier, mark it used.
- A free user with 0 completed quizzes: can take a full quiz
  start to finish (no truncation).
- On completion, increment.
- A free user who already completed 1 quiz: starting any quiz
  shows the upgrade prompt instead (gate at quiz entry/load).
Enforce server-side: the quiz-by-topic endpoint (or an
entitlement check) refuses to serve a 2nd quiz to a free user
who's used their one. Frontend shows upgrade.

(c) EXAMS — 1 completed exam total for free users.
Same pattern as quizzes, separately tracked
(freeExamsCompleted). One full completed exam, then upgrade gate.

(d) STUDY GUIDES — paid-only, shown-but-locked.
Free users: the Study Guide study-mode is shown with a lock icon
and "Unlock with Master". Tapping it shows the upgrade prompt.
Backend study-guide endpoint refuses content to free users (403/
402). Master/Scholar: full access.

(e) SHOWN-BUT-LOCKED treatment:
On topic-detail and study-mode entry points, locked modes for
free users render visibly with a lock + "Unlock with Master"
rather than being hidden. Flashcards always available (capped).
Quiz/Exam available until their one freebie is used, then locked.
Study Guide always locked for free.

COMPLETION semantics: quiz/exam counts as used when the user
reaches the results/score screen, NOT on open — so backing out
mid-attempt doesn't burn the freebie.

If implementing requires schema additions (freeQuizzesCompleted /
freeExamsCompleted columns or small tables), that's in scope —
FLAG the schema change in your plan before applying for review.
