General Personality Disorder 
-An enduring pattern of inner experience and behavior that deviates markedly from the expectations of the individuals culture 
Criterion A: manifested in 2 or more of the following: 
Cognition 
Affectivity 
Interpersonal functioning 
Impulse control 
Criterion B: Enduring pattern is inflexible and pervasive across a broad range of personal and social situations 
Criterion C: Clinically significant distress and impairment in multiple domains  
Criterion D: Long enduring and tracked to childhood 
Criterion E: Not better explained by another mental manifestation/disorder 
Criterion F: Not attributed to substance 
 
Cluster A Personality Disorders 
 
Paranoid Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive distrust and suspiciousness of others that their motive is interpreted as malevolent beginning in early adulthood 
Criterion A: indicated by 4 or more of the following: 
Suspects exploitation, harm, deceit 
Preoccupation with untrustworthiness or loyalty 
Reluctant to confide in others as info will be used maliciously 
Find hidden meaning in remarks/interactions 
Grudges 
Perception of attack to character 
Suspects infidelity 
Criterion B: Doesn’t occur exclusively during another mental disorder (Ex: schizophrenia, bipolar, etc) 
 
Schizoid Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive detachment in social relationships and restricted range of emotional expression in interpersonal settings, beginning in adulthood 
Criterion A: indicated by 4 or more of the following: 
Does not desire/enjoy close relationships 
Solidary activities 
Little, if any interest in sexual activities 
Little pleasure in activities 
Lack close relationships 
Indifferent to praise or criticism 
Emotional coldness/detachment/affectivity 
Criterion B: Doesn’t occur exclusively during another mental disorder (Ex: schizophrenia, bipolar, etc) 
 
Schizotypical Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive social and interpersonal deficits with acute discomfort with close relationships a well as cognitive or perceptual distortions and eccentricities in behavior 
Criterion A: indicated by 5 or more of the following: 
Ideas of reference 
Odd beliefs/magical thinking 
Unusual perceptual experiences/bodily illusions 
Odd thinking and speech 
Suspiciousness or paranoid ideation 
Inappropriate/constricted affect 
Odd, eccentric, peculiar behavior or appearance 
Lack of close relationships 
Excessive social anxiety associated with paranoid features 
Criterion B: Doesn’t occur exclusively during another mental disorder (Ex: schizophrenia, bipolar, etc) 
 
 
Cluster B Personality Disorders 
 
Antisocial Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive disregard and violation of the rights of other, occurring since 15 
Criterion A: indicated by 3 or more of the following: 
Failure to conform to social norms in by means of acts that are grounds for arrest 
Deceitfulness 
Impulsivity/failure to plan 
Irritability/aggressiveness by fighting or assaults 
Reckless with regard to self and others 
Irresponsibility with work and financial obligations 
Lack of remorse 
Criterion B: At least 18 
Criterion C: Conduct disorder before 15 
Criterion D: Not due to schizophrenia or bipolar 
 
Borderline Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive instability in interpersonal relationships, self-image and affects with marked impulsivity, beginning in adulthood 
Criterion A: indicated by 5 or more of the following 
Efforts to avoid abandonment 
Unstable interpersonal relationships with idealization and devaluation 
Identity disturbance 
Impulsivity in 2 or more self-damaging areas: 
Finances 
Sex 
Substance use 
Reckless driving 
Eating 
Recurrent suicidal behavior, gestures, threats, self-mutalization 
Affective instability due to reactive mood 
Emptiness 
Uncontrollable anger 
Paranoid ideation or dissociative symptoms 
 
Histrionic Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive, excessive emotionality and attention-seeking, beginning in adulthood 
Criterion A: indicated by 5 or more of the following 
Uncomfortable when not the center of attention 
Inappropriate sexual behavior with others 
Shifting and shallow emotions 
Physical appearance to draw attention 
Impressionistic style of speech 
Self-dramatization/theatricality 
Suggestible 
Relationships more intimate than they actually are 
 
Narcissistic Personality Disorder 
-Pervasive pattern of grandiosity, need for admiration, lack of empathy, beginning in adulthood 
Criterion A: indicated by 5 or more of the following 
Grandiose sense of self importance 
Preoccupation with fantasies of success/power/love/brilliance 
Believe they are special/unique/high power 
Require excessive admiration 
Entitlement 
Interpersonally exploitative 
Lack of empathy 
Envious of others 
Arrogant behavior or attitude
